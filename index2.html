ABOUT

#AliceGameJam

Explore the world of Alice in Wonderland and her friends virtually with Pocket Code and Scratch at the #AliceGameJam during the Computer Science Education Week between December 7th and December 13th 2015. Create your own program about Alice and get the chance of being part of something bigger.

150 years of Alice in Wonderland

Lewis Carroll’s Alice in Wonderland was published in 1865. Since then, the adventures of Alice, the White Rabbit and the other fabulous figures in the book have fascinated young and old for 150 years. Now, between the 7th and 13th of December 2015, the Alice Game Jam invites you to recreate the spirit of this magical world by creating fun new games about Alice and her friends.

Abstract

Alice follows a white rabbit into its hole and comes to a magical, imaginative, and exciting world. She experiences some gorgeous and thrilling adventures on her journey through this wonderland, meeting new and marvelous creatures like the Mad Hatter or the Cheshire Cat...

With Pocket Code we aim to inspire teenagers to create their own games and interactive stories directly on their smartphones without any previous knowledge in a "Lego-Style" way of programming. What theme for our first Game Jam could be better than Alice in Wonderland?

Pocket Code available for free at

Are you already a Scratch user? Explore the possibilities of Scratch to create your personal story of Alice and her friends. There are thousands of possibilities to design your very own Alice-themed animation or game just within minutes and to share it afterwards with your friends and the whole world.

Get online and bring Alice alive at scratch.mit.edu.

EVENT

Details

The Game Jam will take place during the international Computer Science Education Week from December 7th to December 13th 2015. All information needed will be published on this website during this week. So stay tuned!

Theme

It’s all about Alice in Wonderland! Create your own game and story about her amazing adventures. And don’t forget the White Rabbit, the Cheshire Cat, the Mad Hatter and all the other colourful characters from the book. More details about the theme will be made available before the Jam starts.

Surprise topic:

As a special challenge we will announce a “Surprise Topic” related to Alice at the start of the Game Jam! All this games will be published separately on the wall of fame and beyond all participates of this special theme we will raffle off a special prize.

Rules

You can either take part as a single creator or as a team of up to four people. As a framework to create your game you have to use our free “Pocket Code” app or the free platform Scratch by the MIT Media Lab.

Further information can be found on our Frequently Asked Questions page.

Wall of Fame

All submitted games, stories and animations will be published together on our "Wall of Fame" during and after this Event. So you will get the chance to share your project with your friends, family and our community!

There also will be a raffle in which all participates who submitted an Alice-themed program have the chance to win great prizes and give-aways.

Share this event with your friends!

PARTICIPATE

How to participate?

Decide whether you want to work on your own or in a team; be aware of the surprise theme and the diversifiers that will be announced on December 7, 2015; follow the rules, then start creating your own Pocket Code or Scratch game about “Alice in Wonderland”.

Submission

If you’ve finished your game, upload it at the relevant community (Pocket Code or Scratch) and tag it with #AliceGameJam in the description. Then fill out the participation form that will be made available on this page during the event.

In Cooperation with

GameCity

Based in Nottingham (UK), GameCity has worked for 10 years to explore and celebrate the culture, history and creativity of videogames. Its projects include the annual GameCity Festival, the GameCity Prize, and the new National Videogame Arcade.

If you want to know more about GameCity visit www.gamecity.org.

Catrobat

Catrobat is a non-profit project providing free educational apps for children and teenagers, such as Pocket Code or Pocket Paint.

If you want to know more about Catrobat visit www.catrobat.org.

No One Left Behind project

The “No One Left Behind” project tackles key challenges in the education sector by using the potential of digital games and game-making. The project will unlock inclusive gaming creation and experiences in formal learning situations, underpin meaningful learning, and support children to realise their full potential.

This project has received funding from the European Union’s Seventh Framework Programme for research, technological development and demonstration under grant agreement no 645215.

For further information visit www.no1leftbehind.eu

British Library

The British Library is the largest library of the world by catalogued items and one of the most important in the fields of research. It is also the home of the original Alice in Wonderland books and provides a wide range of resources about Alice and her adventures. The Discovering Literature section provides lots of opportunities to find exciting and new facts about Alice, e.g., Understanding Alice.

The Scratch Foundation

The Scratch Foundation has its specific focus on Scratch and its dynamic ecosystem of interacting projects (Scratch, ScratchJr, ScratchEd) and events (Scratch Day, Scratch Conference, Scratch Educator Meetups).
