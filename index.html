#AliceGameJam

ABOUT

150 years of Alice in Wonderland

Lewis Carroll’s Alice in Wonderland was published in 1865. Since then, the adventures of Alice, the White Rabbit and the other fabulous figures in the book have fascinated young and old for 150 years. Now, between the 7th and 13th of December 2015, the Alice Game Jam invites you to recreate the spirit of this magical world by creating fun new games about Alice and her friends. You don’t need any previous experience producing apps or coding; you don’t even need a PC, laptop, or tablet. Your smartphone is enough, and you’ll learn the rest in our tutorials and by exploring what others have created. You’ll then be able to share your own real game apps with your friends and the whole world!

Alice follows a white rabbit into its hole and comes to a magical, imaginative, and exciting world. She experiences some gorgeous and thrilling adventures on her journey through this wonderland, meeting new and marvelous creatures like the Mad Hatter or the Cheshire Cat...

#AliceGameJam

With Pocket Code we aim to inspire teenagers to create their own games and interactive stories directly on their smartphones. What theme for our first Game Jam could be better than Alice in Wonderland?

Alice’s story is full of fantastic creatures, magical encounters, and an imaginative playground for your own creativity! We invite you to be part of our first Pocket Game Jam and create the most amazing games you can think of!

Share this event with your friends!

Pocket Code is free on Google Play!

EVENT

Details

Stay tuned for further information about the Alice Game Jam! Dates and details will be published shortly on this website.

Theme

It’s all about Alice in Wonderland! Create your own game and story about her amazing adventures. And don’t forget the White Rabbit, the Cheshire Cat, the Mad Hatter and all the other colourful characters from the book. More details about the theme will be made available before the Jam starts.

Surprise topic:

As a special challenge we will announce a “Surprise Topic” related to Alice at the start of the Game Jam!

Rules

You can either take part as a single creator or as a team of up to four people. As a framework to create your game you have to use our “Pocket Code” app, which is freely available on Google Play.

Further information can be found on our Frequently Asked Questions page.

Jury

There will also rewards and prizes for the best games submitted!

An expert jury will select the best games. Further information about the jury will be available before the start of the event.

PARTICIPATE

How to participate?

Decide whether you want to work on your own or in a team; be aware of the surprise theme and the diversifiers that will be announced on December 7; follow the rules, then start creating your own Pocket Code game about “Alice in Wonderland”.

Submission

If you’ve finished your game, upload it by clicking on the upload button within Pocket Code and tag it with #AliceGameJam in the description. Then fill out the participation form that will be made available on this page during the event.

OUR PARTNERS

GameCity

Based in Nottingham (UK), GameCity has worked for 10 years to explore and celebrate the culture, history and creativity of videogames. Its projects include the annual GameCity Festival, the GameCity Prize, and the new National Videogame Arcade.

If you want to know more about GameCity visit www.gamecity.org.

No One Left Behind project

The “No One Left Behind” project tackles key challenges in the education sector by using the potential of digital games and game-making. The project will unlock inclusive gaming creation and experiences in formal learning situations, underpin meaningful learning, and support children to realise their full potential.

This project has received funding from the European Union’s Seventh Framework Programme for research, technological development and demonstration under grant agreement no 645215.

For further information visit www.no1leftbehind.eu

Catrobat

Catrobat is a non-profit project providing free educational apps for children and teenagers.

If you want to know more about Catrobat visit www.catrobat.org.
