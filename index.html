#AliceGameJam

ABOUT

150 years Alice in Wonderland

In 1865 Lewis Carroll published the first book about the stories of Alice in Wonderland. Now 150 years later still everyone is in common with this famous stories about Alice, the White Rabbit and all the other fabulous figures in these books. Especially young girls and boys are fascinated by them, giving them the opportunity to escape in a surreal and wonderful world. We would like to give young minds the opportunity to bring this world onto your phone by creating new imaginative apps about Alice, her friends and stories.

The young girl Alice follows a white rabbit into its hole and comes to a magical, imaginative and exciting world. She experiences some gorgeous and thrilling adventures on her journey through the wonderland. She meets new and marvelous creatures like the White Rabbit, The Mad Hatter and Cheshire cat on her way through the doors of this fascinating world she faced at the beginning of her story..

#AliceGameJam

With Pocket Code we aim to inspire teenagers to create their own games and interactive stories directly on their mobile devices. What theme for our first Pocket Jam could be better than Alice in Wonderland? Alice’ Story is full of fantastic creatures, magical stories and an imaginative playground for your own creativity! We invite you to be part of our first PocketJam and create the most amazing games you can think of!

EVENT

Details

Stay tuned for further information about the Alice Game Jam! Dates and details will be published shortly on this website!

Theme

It’s all about Alice in Wonderland! Create your own game and story about her. Include her friends the White Rabbit, Cheshire Cat and the Mad Hatter to form a marvelous program. More details about it will be available soon enough before the Jam starts!

Surprise topic:

As a special challenge keep our “Surprise Topic” in mind, which will be released just before the Game Jam and will contain some really exciting additional tasks!

Diversifiers:

There also will be some diversifiers, e.g., a game that can be played by one-tap, phone sensors are used within this game, …

Rules

You can either take part as a single creator or as a team up to 4 people. As framework to create your game you have to use our app “Pocket Code”, which is available for free on Google Play.

Additionally please keep the copyright of others in mind, you can get further information about this in our FAQ.

Furthermore all submitted apps have to be suitable for minors, so no violent or inappropriate content will be accepted.

Jury

There will also be some rewards and prizes for the best games submitted!

An expert jury will take a look at all submitted games and elect the best. Further information about the jury will be available just before the event!

PARTICIPATE

How to participate?

If you want to be part of this jam just get together with some friends, take a look at this page and be aware of the theme, diversifiers and rules before you start creating your own Pocket Code game about “Alice in Wonderland”. In the end just tag your uploaded program with the hashtag #AliceGameJam and commit your data at the provided submission form on this page!

We wish you lots of fun and good luck for this jam!

Registration (of teams/single game creators)

To register just fill out the form at http://…. and provide all information needed. Of course we take care of privacy issues, so data just may be used for this jam and internal processes. To get more information about it just check out the “Terms & Privacy” sector, linked at the bottom of this page.

Submission

If you’ve finished your game please upload it by clicking on the upload button within Pocket Code and tag it with #AliceGameJam, your personal title of the App and if present mention copyrights in your description. After that just commit the link in this submission form and that’s it!

No One Left Behind project

The “No One Left Behind” project tackles key challenges in the education sector by using the potential of digital games and game-making. The project will unlock inclusive gaming creation and experiences in formal learning situations, underpin meaningful learning and support children to realise their full potential.

This project has received funding from the European Union’s Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme under grant agreement No 645215

For further information visit www.no1leftbehind.eu

Catrobat

Catrobat is a non-profit project by Graz University of Technology, providing free educational apps for children and teenagers. More over 120 volunteers work on these apps to engage users to create their own programs, games and animation in an easy to use, visual way directly on their smartphone.
