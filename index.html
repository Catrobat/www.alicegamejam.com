ABOUT

#AliceGameJam

Explore the world of Alice in Wonderland and her friends virtually with Pocket Code and Scratch at the #AliceGameJam during the Computer Science Education Week between December 7th and December 13th 2015. Create your own program about Alice and get the chance of being part of something bigger.

150 years of Alice in Wonderland

Lewis Carroll’s Alice in Wonderland was published in 1865. Since then, the adventures of Alice, the White Rabbit and the other fabulous figures in the book have fascinated young and old for 150 years. Now, between the 7th and 13th of December 2015, the Alice Game Jam invites you to recreate the spirit of this magical world by creating fun new games about Alice and her friends.

Alice' Story

Alice follows a white rabbit into its hole and comes to a magical, imaginative, and exciting world. She experiences some gorgeous and thrilling adventures on her journey through this wonderland, meeting new and marvelous creatures like the Mad Hatter or the Cheshire Cat...

With Pocket Code we aim to inspire teenagers to create their own games and interactive stories directly on their smartphones without any previous knowledge in a "Lego-Style" way of programming. What theme for our first Game Jam could be better than Alice in Wonderland?

Pocket Code available for free at

You can use Scratch to create your own stories, games, and animations and share them with others around the world. Visit the Alice in Wonderland Studio to explore project ideas and find the Cheshire Cat and other characters to remix and share.

Get online and bring Alice alive at scratch.mit.edu.



EVENT

Details

The Game Jam will take place during the international Computer Science Education Week from December 7th to December 13th 2015. All information needed will be published on this website during this week. So stay tuned!

Theme

It’s all about Alice in Wonderland! Create your own game and story about her amazing adventures. And don’t forget the White Rabbit, the Cheshire Cat, the Mad Hatter and all the other colourful characters from the book. More details about the theme will be made available before the Jam starts.

Surprise topic

As a special challenge we will announce a “Surprise Topic” related to Alice at the start of the Game Jam!

Rules

You can either take part as a single creator or as a team of up to four people. As a framework to create your game you can use our free “Pocket Code” app or the free web-based Scratch platform by the MIT Media Lab.

Further information can be found on our Frequently Asked Questions page.

Wall of Fame

All submitted games, stories and animations will be published together on our "Wall of Fame" during and after the game jam. So you will get the chance to share your project with your friends, family, and our community!

There also will be a raffle in which all participates who submitted an Alice-themed program have the chance to win great prizes and give-aways.

Share this event with your friends!

PARTICIPATE

How to participate?

Decide whether you want to work on your own or in a team, be aware of the surprise theme and the diversifiers that will be announced on December 7, 2015; follow the rules, then start creating your own Pocket Code or Scratch game about “Alice in Wonderland”.

Submission

If you’ve finished your game, upload it at the relevant community (Pocket Code or Scratch) and tag it with #AliceGameJam in the description. Then fill out the participation form that will be made available on this page during the event.

How to learn coding?

If you don't have any experience in coding don't be afraid, it's easier as you might think! In the tutorials section we provide some great examples for beginners and lessons to learn programming. Furthermore there are huge communities behind Pocket Code and Scratch which provide examples on the web.

Find the right materials for your game

To make it easier for you we will provide some special media assets which you may use during the Game Jam. To get more information about free media-sources take a look at our FAQs.
